Who We Are

Done Being Polite About It

BAAC is an independent, grassroots coalition of Bay Area residents, community organizers, and advocates. We fight to protect the most vulnerable — especially unhoused people being exploited, trafficked, and recruited into criminal networks.

We don't take government money. We don't answer to the political establishment. We answer to the communities being harmed.

"Progressive policies have failed the Bay Area. The orgs and programs meant to help are enabling the very crisis they claim to solve." — BAAC Founding Statement

What We Fight For

Our Mission

We are targeting the root causes — not just the symptoms — of the Bay Area's crisis.

01

Expose Enabling Programs

Document and expose how government programs and nonprofits are enabling drug use and criminal networks — giving resources to the same people exploiting unhoused communities and making the crisis worse.

02

End Trafficking & Exploitation

Fight for real enforcement against the gangs, traffickers, and drug networks preying on unhoused people across the Bay Area. The most vulnerable deserve protection, not enablement.

03

Demand Accountability

Hold elected officials, law enforcement, and institutions publicly accountable when they fail to act. Name names. Demand answers. Push for consequences.

04

Build Real Coalition

Build a community coalition that represents the residents who've been ignored — the flatlands, the neighborhoods bearing the real cost of failed policies, the people left out of the political conversation.
