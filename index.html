Who We Are

Done Being Polite About It

BAAC is an independent, grassroots coalition of Bay Area residents, community organizers, and advocates fed up with the status quo. Progressive nonprofits and government programs have spent billions enabling addiction, ignoring crime, and making homelessness worse.

We fight for mandatory treatment over enablement, real enforcement over soft prosecution, and communities that are actually safe — not just managed into permanent dysfunction.

"Progressive policies have failed the Bay Area. The orgs and programs meant to help are enabling the very crisis they claim to solve." — BAAC Founding Statement

What We Fight For

Our Mission

We are targeting the root causes — not just the symptoms — of the Bay Area's crisis.

01

Stop Enabling Addiction

Government programs and nonprofits are handing out resources that fuel drug addiction rather than treat it. We expose the orgs and policies actively making the addiction crisis worse under the guise of harm reduction.

02

Community Awareness on Crime

Serious crimes are happening across the Bay Area with little to no accountability — enabled by California's laws, soft prosecutors, and a system that protects criminals over victims. We raise awareness and demand justice.

03

Demand Accountability

Hold elected officials, prosecutors, and institutions publicly accountable when they fail to act. Name names. Demand answers. Push for real consequences — not just statements.

04

Address Root Causes

Homelessness, addiction, and crime are deeply connected. We push for approaches that actually treat addiction, get people off the streets, and break the cycle — instead of policies that entrench it.
